How much does it cost?

Participating is completely free! We will provide food, snacks, and drinks to energize you throughout the day.

Do I have to be a programmer?

Absolutely not! We encourage women with all skill sets to participate. It doesn't matter if you are an artist, a journalist, or a programmer, there is a place for your skillset here.

Do I have to have a team to register?

Nope! [[name]] is a great place to meet new people with different skillsets. You can come with a great project idea or a burning desire to help someone else's idea along. At the beginning of the hackathon, you will definitely have a chance to find people to work with you.

What is "hacking"?

hack [hak]
verb
to modify (a computer program or electronic device) or write (a program) in a skillful or clever way

"Hacking" doesn't mean malicious programming or breaking into something. We want you to "hack" (create, build, combine, MacGyver) technology, art, and media together to create something awesome. (We don't want you getting in trouble with the law!)

Who can participate?

We welcome all current CU students from any department or major. We encourage all students who self-identify as female to participate. This includes students of all genders who were assigned female at birth, or those who identify as female now.

What do I have to bring?

Bring your laptop, phone, chargers, and a well-rested open mind.
